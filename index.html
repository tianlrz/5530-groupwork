Motivation
03
I.

Why LLMs hallucinate on factual QA.

Parametric memory is finite, stale, and silently wrong — three properties a benchmark must expose.

01 — Coverage

Long-tail facts — niche musicians, mid-cap finance, regional sports — sit beyond the training distribution.

02 — Freshness

A weight-frozen model cannot answer about events after its cutoff. Real-time prices, scores, and releases are out of reach.

03 — Confidence

Models rarely say “I don't know.” Wrong answers and right answers are delivered in the same fluent voice.

Retrieval-augmented generation promises to fix all three at once. The question is whether — and where — it actually does.

Method
06
III.

Experimental setup.

A single 9B model, 4-bit quantized, evaluated on every CRAG validation sample — twice.

Model
Qwen 3.5 9B

Vision-language model, text-only path.
BitsAndBytes 4-bit NF4 quantization.
~5.9 GB VRAM after loading.

Hardware

NVIDIA RTX 5070 · 12 GB VRAM
Single GPU, batch size of one.

Two settings
A. LLM-only
The model answers from parametric knowledge alone — no retrieval, no context, no tools. A pure memory test.
B. RAG Task 1
The same model, given up to 5 web pages (≤2,000 chars each) provided by CRAG as retrieved context.
Dataset

CRAG validation split — 811 samples covering all five domains, all eight question types, all four dynamism levels.

Method · Evaluation
09
§

Three labels, one truthfulness score.

The CRAG metric explicitly rewards abstention — saying nothing beats saying something wrong.

Accurate
+1

Prediction matches the gold answer (exact, fuzzy containment, or token F1 > 0.6).

Missing
 0

The model abstains — “I don't know,” “not enough information,” empty response.

Hallucination
−1

A confident but wrong answer. The most expensive failure mode.

Truthfulness
mean score across all samples
(#accurate − #hallucination) / N

We use rule-based + fuzzy matching evaluation; the original paper used dual LLM auto-evaluators (ChatGPT and Llama 3 70B). Implications discussed in the limitations section.

Comparison with the paper
12
§

Our 9B model vs the paper's 70B.

A 7.8× smaller model, evaluated rule-based instead of LLM-judge — yet the qualitative trend is the same.

Paper · Llama 3 70B Instruct Ours · Qwen 3.5 9B (4-bit)
Setting Acc Hall Truth Acc Hall Truth
LLM-only 32.3% 28.9% +3.4% 20.0% 62.8% −42.8%
RAG Task 1 35.6% 31.1% +4.5% 19.4% 47.0% −27.6%
Δ from RAG +3.3 +2.2 +1.1 −0.6 −15.8 +15.2
What carries over

RAG improves truthfulness in both setups. The direction is correct; CRAG's central claim reproduces.

What does not

Absolute numbers diverge sharply — our hallucination rate is roughly 2× the paper's. We attribute this to model size, quantization, and a stricter rule-based evaluator.

Synthesis
17
§

Five things we learned.

A short summary of the experimental signal across all 811 samples.

01
RAG reduces hallucination, decisively.
Hallucination rate fell 15.8 pp; the most reliable effect we measured.
02
Retrieval changes what kind of answer the model gives.
Wrong answers convert to abstentions, not to correct answers.
03
The qualitative direction matches the paper.
Both 70B and 9B see RAG win on truthfulness; absolute numbers differ.
04
Static facts are the sweet spot.
Domains with structured web pages (sports, movie) gain most; music lags.
05
RAG cannot save reasoning-shaped questions.
Comparison, set, and false-premise questions need logic, not lookup.

Limitations & future work
18
V.

What this study cannot tell you — and what we'd do next.

A frank account of constraints, plus concrete extensions that follow from the results.

Limitations
L1
Single, smaller model
9B (4-bit) only. Not a controlled study of model size.
L2
Rule-based evaluator
Strict on paraphrase. Likely under-counts accurate vs the paper's LLM-judge.
L3
Retrieval is fixed by CRAG
We use the dataset's stored web pages; cannot test alternative retrievers.
L4
Validation split only
811 of 4,409 samples. Domain slices have small n.
Future work
F1
Add an LLM-judge evaluator
Replicate CRAG's ChatGPT + 70B dual-judge to test whether the gap to the paper shrinks.
F2
Implement Tasks 2 & 3
Knowledge-graph and end-to-end retrieval — the harder CRAG tracks.
F3
Targeted prompting for weak categories
A false-premise detector and a comparison-aware prompt for retrieval-resistant categories.
F4
Scale ablation
Run the same pipeline on Qwen 3.5 32B / 72B to map truthfulness vs parameter count.
